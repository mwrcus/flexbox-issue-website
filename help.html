INDIVIDUALLY, YOU SHOULD...

Use Alternative Transportation

Reduce car usage. Consider biking, walking, and using public transportation when possible. Carpooling also works.

Reduce Energy Consumption

Use energy-efficient appliances and LED lighting. Make sure your home is well insulated.

Switch to Renewable

Considering using electric technology, appliances, and equipment. You may also benefit long-term by installing solar panels on the roof of your home.

Support Sustainability

Purchase low-carbon footprint products and support eco-friendly companies. Make sure to reduce, reuse, and recycle!

Advocate for Change

Support local and national policies and officials that promote clean energy and a brighter future. Encourage the people you surround yourself with to take eco-friendly actions.

AS A COMMUNITY, WE SHOULD...

Better the Infrastructure

Promote a greener infrastructure: build up, use energy-efficient material, and remove roads. Develop public transportation and more green spaces. Cities are meant for the people, not cars.

Provide Incentives

Provide EV adoption incentives through charging stations and tax benefits.

Adopt Green Energy

Make energy grids more efficient, and fully adopt renewable sources (solar, wind, water, and geothermal).

Educate the Public

Residents should be educated about the importance of air pollution and fossil fuel use. Information should be provided such as on incentives and accessible data about the air quality index.
